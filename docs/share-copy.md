## One-Line Project Description

Two voice UX prototypes exploring when AI should speak less: a bedtime disengagement companion and a pause-aware interview facilitator.

## Short Description

Humlab Voice Agent Cases is a pair of live prototypes about restraint in voice interaction. `Good Night` explores how an AI voice can help someone leave an interaction before sleep through low-stimulation pacing, persona design, and gradual audio fade. `Interview Facilitator` explores the opposite condition: how a voice agent can stay present without closing unfinished thinking too early.

The project asks a simple question: what if a voice agent's best design move is knowing when to stop talking?

## Social Post

What if a voice agent's best design move is knowing when to stop talking?

I built two small Voice UX prototypes around that question:

- `Good Night`: a bedtime voice experience designed to fade out of the user's attention
- `Interview Facilitator`: a pause-aware voice agent for protecting thinking-in-progress

Together they explore timing, silence, restraint, and designed withdrawal in AI interaction.
